Summer Plumbing Tips for Greater Lansing Homes

Sprinklers 4 min read

Spring sprinkler startup, done right

Before you fire up your irrigation system, open the main shutoff slowly to avoid water hammer that can crack pipes. Run each zone for a minute or two. Watch for soggy spots, surprise geysers, or heads that don't pop up — those signal underground leaks or breaks.

Can't find the source? Call us. We'll locate it fast.

Outdoor Faucets 3 min read

Hose bib & outdoor faucet check

A faucet that drips after you turn it off — or water running inside the wall when the bib is off — usually means a worn washer or a cracked pipe from last winter's freeze. A tiny drip in June becomes a wall full of mold by August.

Fix it early, save the drywall.

Summer Storms 2 min read

Summer storms and your sump pump

Mid-Michigan storms can dump inches in an hour. Test your sump pump now: pour a bucket of water into the pit and watch it cycle. If it doesn't start, or cycles slowly, schedule service before the next thunderstorm.

Ask us about battery backups — most failures happen during power outages.
