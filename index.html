Free advice

Seasonal 4 min read

Winter pipe protection for Lansing homes

Before the first hard freeze, disconnect outside hoses. During deep cold snaps, let a pencil-thin trickle run from the faucet furthest from your main. Know where your shut-off valve is before you need it. Open cabinet doors under sinks on the coldest nights.

Still frozen? Don't use open flame. Call us.

Water Heaters 3 min read

Signs you need a new water heater

Lukewarm water where there used to be hot. Rusty or cloudy water from the hot tap. Popping, rumbling, or knocking sounds from the tank. Any unit older than ten years. Water pooling around the base.

If two of these sound familiar, plan before it fails.

Drains 2 min read

When a clog becomes an emergency

A slow sink is inconvenient. Sewage smells indoors, multiple backed-up drains at once, gurgling after the washer runs, or a toilet that won't flush even with a plunger — those usually mean the main sewer line, not a DIY problem.

Stop running water. Call a plumber.
